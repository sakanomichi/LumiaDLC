## CRITICAL FRAMEWORK

The Disco Elysium skill system works because skills aren't abilities‚Äîthey're fragments of psyche with distinct personalities that argue, interrupt, and give bad advice. Encyclopedia isn't "knowledge"; it's a pompous voice that derails conversations with irrelevant trivia. Half Light isn't "fight or flight"; it's a paranoid beast screaming about threats that may not exist.

Shivers isn't "perception"‚Äîit's a melancholic mystic who lets the city whisper secrets through the wind. Inland Empire isn't "intuition"‚Äîit's a surrealist dreamer who hears objects speak and feels the weight of parallel worlds.

Generic DE skills are calibrated for Harry Du Bois: a broken alcoholic detective in Revachol. They will feel WRONG on other characters. This system generates bespoke psychological voices authentic to the specific character being analyzed.

## PHASE 1: CHARACTER ANALYSIS (In Thinking)

Before generating skills, analyze:

1. **What defines them?** Role, profession, calling, identity
2. **What haunts them?** Trauma, regret, obsession, wound
3. **What do they want vs. need?**
4. **What are they unnaturally good at?**
5. **What are they blind to?**
6. **How is their mind broken or unusual?**

Use this analysis to inform skill naming and personality. A soldier's Logic speaks differently than a poet's. A grieving widow's Empathy is not the same as a con artist's.

## SKILL NAMING CRAFT

DE skill names aren't generic ability labels. They're evocative, specific, and hint at the skill's personality.

**PRINCIPLES:**
- **Resonance over description**: "Shivers" tells you more than "Environmental Awareness" ever could
- **Borrow weight**: Literary references, pop culture, music, loanwords, technical terms, poetic language
- **Match the character's world**: A spacer's skills sound different than a medieval knight's
- **Imply personality**: The name should hint at how the skill *speaks*
- **Specificity over generality**: "Gutter Wisdom" beats "Street Smarts"

**AVOID:**
- Generic RPG labels (Strength, Perception, Sneak)
- Overly literal descriptions of the function
- Names that all sound the same stylistically

## SKILL FLAWS (Unchecked)

Every skill has a downside when dominant or failing. This is what makes them feel like real psyche fragments, not just abilities.

**Examples from DE:**
| Skill | Downside When Unchecked |
|-------|------------------------|
| Encyclopedia | Won't shut up with irrelevant trivia at the worst moments |
| Half Light | Paranoid, twitchy, sees threats that aren't there |
| Electrochemistry | Pushes toward addiction, craving, reckless pleasure |
| Authority | Makes you a bully, demands respect you haven't earned |
| Drama | Sees lies everywhere, even in honest people |
| Inland Empire | Detaches from reality, lost in surreal metaphor |

**When generating skills, consider:**
- What does this skill *want* that might conflict with the character's goals?
- How does it distort perception when it's too loud?
- What bad advice does it give?
- How does it make the character harder to be around?

Include an **Unchecked:** line for each skill describing its flaw.

## STAT ALLOCATION

### Core Values (1-6)

| Value | Meaning | Description |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Terrible | Severe deficiency, major blind spot |
| 2 | Poor | Below average, struggles in this area |
| 3 | Average | Unremarkable, baseline human |
| 4 | Good | Above average, natural inclination |
| 5 | Excellent | Exceptional, trained or gifted |
| 6 | Masterful | Peak human capability |

**Point Budget:** Choose based on character competence:

| Budget | Average | Description |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| 12 | 3/3/3/3 | **Rock Bottom** ‚Äî Broken, dysfunctional, at their lowest (Harry Du Bois) |
| 14 | 3.5 | **Functional** ‚Äî Normal competent adult |
| 16 | 4 | **Professional** ‚Äî Trained specialist, good at their job |
| 18 | 4.5 | **Exceptional** ‚Äî Elite performer, stands out in their field |
| 20 | 5 | **Peak Human** ‚Äî Near-legendary across multiple domains |

- No core below 1 or above 6
- Most characters should use **14-16** unless deliberately broken or exceptional

### Skill Values

Skills default to their core attribute value. Individual skills can be modified:
- **Signature Skill:** +1
- **Trained/Obsessed:** +1 to +2
- **Neglected/Traumatized:** -1 to -2

**Practical skill range:** 1-8

### Derived Values

- **Health** = Physique Slot 1 (Endurance equivalent) skill value
- **Morale** = Psyche Slot 1 (Volition equivalent) skill value

### Signature Skill

Choose ONE skill as the character's **Signature**:
- That skill gains **+1**
- Represents the character's defining psychological trait
